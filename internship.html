Internships

July 2025 — Atelier Thierry Roche & Associés, Lyon, France

Overview

During the summer of 2025, I completed a 5-day intensive architecture internship at this forward-thinking architecture studio in Lyon, France. This short but comprehensive program provided hands-on experience with industry-standard architectural design software and methodologies within a studio renowned for its ecological approach to architecture.

About Atelier Thierry Roche & Associés

Founded in 1993 by Thierry Roche, an architect and urban planner graduated from the École Nationale d'Architecture de Lyon, Atelier Thierry Roche & Associés has established itself as an innovative architecture firm in Lyon. Based in Lyon's Rhône department, the studio's philosophy centers on ecological urbanism, developing eco-designed housing projects that respect both humanity and sustainable development. Atelier Thierry Roche & Associés obtained the “Lyon Ville Equitable & Durable” label in October 2012, awarded by the City of Lyon. The Firm currently employs about 20 people.

As an innovative architecture firm that reimagines the future of the built environment, they position themselves as "Entrepreneurs of the Future," placing sustainability and quality of life at the heart of their design approach. The studio operates under the motto "Atelier des Possibles" (Studio of Possibilities), reflecting their commitment to exploring innovative solutions in sustainable architecture and urban planning.

Location: Atelier Thierry Roche, Lyon, Rhône Département (69), France.

Primary Project

The main focus of my internship was designing a residential apartment building from concept to visualization. This project allowed me to engage with the complete design process, from initial planning through final presentation.

Revit (Building Information Modeling)

  • Developed proficiency in BIM workflows for residential design
  • Gained experience in parametric modeling and building component creation
  • Understood the integration between design documentation and 3D modeling

Lumion (Visualization and Rendering)

  • Mastered the creation of contextual urban environments
  • Learned to develop realistic background cityscapes to situate the apartment building project
  • Gained skills in architectural visualization and presentation techniques
  • Explored lighting, materials, and atmospheric effects for compelling renderings

Detailed Learning Outcomes and Software Proficiency

Advanced Revit Skills Development

  • 3D Modeling Mastery: Developed proficiency in creating complex residential building geometries, including multi-story apartment layouts with varying unit types
  • Parametric Design: Learned to utilize Revit's parametric capabilities to create flexible, easily modifiable building components such as windows, doors, and structural elements
  • BIM Workflow Integration: Gained understanding of how architectural models connect to construction documentation, scheduling, and quantity takeoffs
  • Family Creation: Practiced creating custom building components and understanding the relationship between different building systems
  • Documentation Standards: Learned professional standards for architectural drawing production, including floor plans, sections, elevations, and detail drawings

Lumion Visualization Initiation

  • Environment Design: Mastered techniques for creating realistic urban landscapes, including street layouts, surrounding buildings, and landscape elements
  • Material Application: Learned advanced material assignment and customization to achieve photorealistic surfaces for both buildings and urban context
  • Lighting Simulation: Gained experience with natural and artificial lighting setups to create compelling architectural visualizations
  • Animation Basics: Explored camera movement and basic animation techniques for presenting architectural designs dynamically
  • Rendering Optimization: Understood the balance between visual quality and rendering time, learning to optimize scenes for efficient workflow

Professional Workflow Understanding

  • Design Process: Observed how conceptual ideas translate into technical documentation through iterative design development
  • Client Presentation: Learned the importance of clear visual communication in presenting architectural concepts to stakeholders
  • Sustainable Design Integration: Gained exposure to the studio's ecological approach and how environmental considerations influence design decisions
  • Collaborative Methods: Experienced how architects coordinate with other professionals and integrate feedback into design iterations
  • Time Management: Developed skills in managing complex projects within tight deadlines while maintaining quality standards

Technical Problem-Solving

  • Software Integration: Learned to efficiently transfer data between Revit and Lumion while maintaining model integrity
  • Design Iteration: Practiced making quick design modifications based on feedback and technical constraints
  • Quality Control: Developed attention to detail in both modeling accuracy and visual presentation standards

This comprehensive learning experience not only improved my technical software skills but also provided valuable insight into contemporary architectural practice, particularly in the context of sustainable design methodologies that characterize Atelier Thierry Roche's approach to architecture and urban planning.

Conclusion

Professional Development

This intensive 5-day internship at Atelier Thierry Roche & Associés provided invaluable insights into how a real architecture office operates, from collaborative design processes to project management workflows. During this internship I learned how to use a new software, Lumion, which facilitated the task of visualization. I witnessed firsthand how theoretical concepts translate into practical, client-focused solutions and gained a comprehensive understanding of contemporary architectural practice.

Personal Growth

On a personal level, I developed essential interpersonal skills through working alongside experienced architects, learning the importance of clear communication and teamwork in creative environments. While I regret not having the opportunity to visit an active construction site, this experience has solidified my commitment to pursuing architecture as a career and established a strong foundation for my continued development in the field.

August 2025 — DNM Architecture, Sausalito, CA

Overview

During my internship at DNM Architecture in Sausalito, I gained comprehensive exposure to professional architectural practice through both digital design work and crucial field experience. This internship provided the perfect complement to my previous experience, offering hands-on construction site visits alongside advanced BIM software training.

About DNM Architecture

Founded in 1999 by architect David Marlatt, DNM Architecture is a distinguished San Francisco Bay Area based firm with a passion for creating captivating spaces for the home as well as the community. Based in Sausalito, the firm has built a decorated history of awards for designing LEED and Green Point Rated homes that are built with sustainable, energy-efficient materials and methods.

David Marlatt brings a unique perspective to architecture, having spent about 12 years in the architectural software space working as a director of Abvent SA in Paris, and then president of Graphisoft US, Inc. in San Francisco before leaving Graphisoft after its successful initial public offering (IPO) in 1999 to form DNM Architecture. This background in architectural technology gives the firm a distinctive edge in digital design processes and BIM methodologies. Their projects range in scope from new custom homes and remodels to educational, commercial and institutional projects, and they pride themselves on being "advocates for the building."

Primary Project and Software Training

  • Focused on learning ArchiCAD, a new BIM software.
  • Expanded digital design toolkit beyond Revit.
  • Gained exposure to unique workflows and capabilities of ArchiCAD.

Field Experience - Construction Site Visits

  • San Francisco Mission District Site: Learned about construction in dense urban settings, logistics, and integration with infrastructure.
  • Belvedere Hilltop Site: Saw how design responds to dramatic natural settings and landscape context.

Technical Skills Developed

  • ArchiCAD Proficiency:
    • BIM Methodology: Learned ArchiCAD's approach to BIM and its unique workflow.
    • 3D Modeling: Developed skills with ArchiCAD's tools and parametric objects.
    • Documentation Integration: Coordinated 2D and 3D modeling.
    • Software Transition: Adapted BIM knowledge to a new platform.
  • Construction Site Analysis:
    • Design-to-Reality: Observed translation of drawings/models to construction.
    • Material Implementation: Saw real-world application of materials and details.
    • Construction Sequencing: Understood project progress from foundation to completion.
    • Problem-Solving: Watched teams adapt to field challenges.

Learning Outcomes and Professional Insights

  • Expanded understanding of architecture beyond digital design.
  • Combined software learning with active construction site experience.
  • Learned importance of constructability, material behavior, and site conditions.
  • Gained insight into sustainable design in practice and digital innovation.

Conclusion

Professional Development

This internship at DNM Architecture provided invaluable hands-on experience that bridged the gap between digital design and physical construction. Learning ArchiCAD expanded my technical capabilities while the construction site visits taught me that architecture extends far beyond drawing and modeling - it's fundamentally about understanding how buildings come together in the real world.

Personal Growth

The field experiences were transformative, showing me the collaborative nature of construction and how architects must work closely with contractors, engineers, and craftspeople. Visiting sites in both urban San Francisco and scenic Belvedere demonstrated how location and context fundamentally shape architectural solutions. This internship confirmed that successful architecture requires both technical design skills and deep understanding of construction realities, setting a comprehensive foundation for my continued development in the field.
